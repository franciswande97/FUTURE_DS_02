# FUTURE_DS_02
# Customer Churn & Retention Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the Future Interns Data Science & Analytics Internship Program.

The objective was to analyse customer churn behaviour, identify retention trends, explore customer lifetime patterns, and uncover factors influencing customer retention within a telecommunications business.

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel for data preparation and Power BI for dashboard development and business insight generation.

---

## Business Objectives

The project aimed to answer the following questions:

- What is the overall customer churn rate?
- How effectively is the business retaining customers?
- Which customer segments are most likely to churn?
- How does customer tenure influence retention?
- Which customer cohorts are most at risk?
- What factors contribute to customer churn and long-term retention?

## Key Insights

### Contract Type Drives Retention
Customers on month-to-month contracts experienced significantly higher churn rates compared to customers on one-year and two-year contracts.

### Early Lifecycle Customers Are Most Vulnerable
Newer customer cohorts showed higher churn rates, suggesting that retention initiatives should focus heavily on the early stages of the customer journey.

### Payment Behaviour Matters
Electronic check users displayed higher churn rates than customers using alternative payment methods.

### Internet Service Influences Churn
Churn behaviour varied across internet service categories, highlighting service type as an important customer retention factor.

### Loyalty Increases With Tenure
Customers with longer tenure generally demonstrated stronger retention behaviour than newer customers.
